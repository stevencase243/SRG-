Welcome to Sim Racers Group

SRG is a sim racing league built around hard, clean, and respectful competition. Whether you're in Rookie, F4, or F3, the goal is the same — race hard, race smart, and make it fun for everyone on the grid.

Before you can take part in any SRG race, you need to read and acknowledge our league rules and officiating procedures. This takes just a few minutes and ensures every driver is on the same page.

1

General Philosophy

SRG leagues are built around hard, respectful racing. The goal of officiating is to protect clean racing, encourage improvement, and address behavior that repeatedly harms other members' races.

1.1SRG wants racers to race.
1.2Not every incident will result in a penalty. Some incidents will be considered just racing.
1.3Officials will look first at whether an incident was egregious, whether a review was requested, and whether a driver is showing a repeated pattern of avoidable incidents.
1.4The focus of officiating is not punishment for every mistake. The focus is maintaining a fun, respectful, and reasonably clean racing environment.
1.5Drivers are expected to know the league's on-track conduct guidelines and make reasonable, respectful decisions when racing others.
2

Review Process

2.1Officials may review all races in replay after the event.
2.2Not every contact or mistake will result in action. Small or isolated incidents may be recorded as just racing.
2.3 A review is more likely to result in action if:
  • the incident is egregious
  • a driver requests review
  • the incident causes significant harm
  • a driver shows a repeated pattern of similar conduct
2.4 In reviewing an incident, officials may consider:
  • what happened
  • what each driver saw or could reasonably be expected to see
  • the point in the sequence where the incident first became avoidable
  • whether there were unusual circumstances such as weather, visibility, or rejoining
  • the severity of the outcome
  • whether this was an isolated mistake or part of a larger pattern
  • whether prior coaching or penalties are already in play
2.5If asked for an account of an incident, drivers should respond promptly and concisely.
3

Privacy & Communication

3.1A driver's disciplinary standing will not be publicly disclosed by league admins.
3.2Admins do not want drivers publicly singled out or put on display for disciplinary matters.
3.3In some cases, incidents may be discussed publicly for educational purposes if no formal discipline beyond a conversation or coaching moment was issued.
3.4Admins reserve the right to adjust officiating standards and penalty systems. The default is to make major changes between seasons whenever possible.
4

Penalty Point Guidelines

Penalty points may be assigned when officials determine a driver's actions went beyond normal racing mistakes or created avoidable harm.

PointsDescription
0Self-spin, self-inflicted off-track, harmless contact, or an incident that only harms your own race. A safe rejoin with no harm to others is also 0 points.
1Minor contact, wheel banging, or an incident caused by over-aggression or lack of awareness where both drivers continue without significant damage or major time loss.
2A spin, mistake, or loss of control that causes another driver to take significant avoiding action or lose measurable time, but does not seriously damage or end their race.
3A mistake, bad rejoin, failure to hold brakes, or avoidable contact that causes significant harm to another driver's race, including damage, a spin with unavoidable contact, or sending another driver to pit lane.
4A highly aggressive or careless action that causes a major crash and significantly harms the races of a few drivers.
5Egregious over-aggression or conduct that causes a major incident affecting many drivers.

6

Rookie League

6.1Rookie league does not use the same season-long consequence table.
6.2Rookie is intended to be a place to learn, improve, and race without excessive fear of formal punishment.
6.3Damage is off and Rookie is not a points championship, so the emphasis is on coaching and development.
6.4That said, repeated over-driving, repeated avoidable incidents, or egregious behavior will still be addressed.
6.5Drivers may be coached, held back from promotion, or subject to further action if those issues are not corrected.
7

Final Notes

7.1SRG wants fun, competitive racing in a respectful environment.
7.2Drivers are encouraged to race hard, but also to race with awareness, judgment, and respect for the people around them.
7.3This system is expected to evolve over time, and member feedback is welcome at the end of each season.
Step 3 of 5
1

General Conduct

1.1Race with respect. This is for fun, not win-at-all-costs.
1.2Avoid overly aggressive moves that rely on another driver taking evasive action to avoid contact.
1.3Serve slow-down penalties off the racing line. No blocking or defending.
1.4Follow iRacing track limits.
2

Passing

Establishing Position

2.1Avoid contact. Both drivers share responsibility.
2.2Overlap (alongside) exists when a car's front nose is ahead of the front edge of the defending car's rear tire.
2.3If overlap exists, both drivers should leave racing room. Allow additional margin where possible to account for netcode and latency.
2.4No overlap by turn-in = the trailing car should yield.

Passing Driver Responsibilities

2.5The passing driver is responsible for attempting a safe pass.
2.6A passing attempt should not rely on the other driver taking evasive action to avoid contact.
2.7Do not leave the racing surface to complete a pass.

Defending Driver Responsibilities

2.8Drivers may choose a defensive line before the braking zone but should not make reactive moves in response to a passing attempt. You are expected to maintain a predictable line into corner entry.
2.9Lapped drivers should not make a passing attempt unnecessarily difficult.
3

Rejoining

3.1Use a spotter or blind-spot tool when rejoining or exiting pits.
3.2Rejoin parallel to the track at a safe speed. Do not cross the racing line unsafely — reverse if needed.
3.3Do not interfere with cars on track. Be predictable until up to pace.
3.4Yield to all cars before rejoining. If you cause an incident while rejoining, it is your responsibility.
3.5If you lose control, hold the brakes and avoid unpredictable movement.
3.6Only rejoin once the car is under control.
